Hi, I'm Anthony Chow

I’m a third-year at the University of California, Davis pursuing an individual major in Human-Computer Interaction. Currently, I am a project mentor with Design Interactive—the first student-run, human-centered design consultancy at UC Davis—and was a part of the first cohort of designers. I am also the Co-President of Pilipinx Americans in Science & Engineering (PASE), a student organization catering to Pilipinx folx in STEM related majors. I have previously been the Communications Coordinator for the University Honors Program and First-Year Seminars at UC Davis where I created graphics, flyers, handbooks, and other digital content.

I am passionate about using human-centered design as a form of problem solving and digital story-telling. My journey started with me being interested in digital media and design since middle school, where I loved to make videos and play around with Adobe Photoshop and Illustrator. I came into UC Davis as a Computer Science major but felt stagnant, so after many difficult conversations and reflection I decided to create an individual major that combined all of the disciplines I was interested in including design, computer science, psychology, and statistics. I work closely with a few professors who serve as my major advisors as I navigate through my individual major.

Through my experiences so far, I’ve learned so much about the design process and gaining new perspectives on problem solving through technical, design, and user-centered perspectives. I aim to take advantage of the interdisciplinary nature of my major and be able to utilize these multidisciplinary perspectives to find unique and effective solutions. Additionaly, I strive to continue serving and representing underserved and underrepresented communities as they continue to empower and strengthen me both in and out of academia.
